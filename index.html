Usenet

"Usenet is a worldwide distributed Internet discussion system. It was developed from the general purpose UUCP dial-up network architecture, but subsequently switched to NNTP as usenet grew large.

Usenet was originally created to distribute text content encoded in the 7-bit ASCII character set. With the help of programs that encode 8-bit values into ASCII, it became practical to distribute binary files as content."

-Wikipedia

Providers

Usenet Providers are divided into two sub categories, resellers and Tier-one backend providers. The backend providers own servers connected to Usenet while resellers lease or buy bulk usage from the backend providers.

While resellers can differ in price, location and retention they share the content of their backend provider, something worth taking into consideration.

Retention

"Each newsgroup is generally allocated a certain amount of storage space for post content. When this storage has been filled, each time a new post arrives, old posts are deleted to make room for the new content.

If the network bandwidth available to a server is high but the storage allocation is small, it is possible for a huge flood of incoming content to overflow the allocation and push out everything that was in the group before it."

-Wikipedia
